In 1904 Pablo Ruiz Picasso is 23 years old and already  establishing himself as an artistic force. The title of this painting La Celistina  refers to a character from Comedia de Calisto Imelevia. In English tragic  comedy of Calisto Imelevia. Written in 1499 it is considered one of the greatest  works of Spanish literature. La Celistina is a portrait of an actual woman from Barcelona  whose name was Calote Vallevia and she was a neighborhood figure. He portrayed her in  several drawings and always shows her as a kind of shadowy and even sinister figure. This  is one of the most finished portraits of this period and it doesn't have an  element of pathos or that kind of pathetic down trotting quality. She's got a real  watchful almost cat-like sensibility as she sidles into the picture. The blue palette  prevails here but La Celistina also features one of Picasso's artistic hallmarks  unique eyes. A trait you'll see in works throughout the exhibition. La Celistina's  right eye stares at you with the deep intensity. While the left one is covered  with a hazy film that some scholars interpret as a cataract.
